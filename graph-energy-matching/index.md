## Overview

Graph Energy Matching (GEM) is a generative framework for graphs that learns a permutation-invariant energy function for guiding samples from noise toward data-like graphs and refining them near high-likelihood regions.

The method is designed for discrete graph domains where explicit relative likelihoods are useful, including conditional generation, constraint-aware sampling, property-guided exploration, and interpolation between graphs.
